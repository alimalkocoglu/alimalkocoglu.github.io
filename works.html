The Product

SHFT Tribes are online group coaching products that are based on video and text communication guided by life coaches. Topics include self love, anxiety, depression etc.s

My Role

I joined the SHFT team at an early stage as UX and UI designer. I worked closely with the product manager and managed the design efforts for the entire product experience from scratch in addition to working with the marketing team with user acquisition efforts.

Challenge - Objective

After increasing the conversion rates for different SHFT products, (which can be found in other work examples) the team and I shifted our focus to increase the retention rate.

Discovery - User Research

I was inspired by Facebook and Twitter design teams’ approach to finding what makes a user come back and repurchase. In order to deconstruct the path that leads to becoming a power user, I closely looked at SHFT’s existing power users behaviour: I examined which SHFT products they have purchased, and how they behave / interact with the others inside online group. I also analyzed SHFT’s most popular products (in this case online life coaching classes) to see if they exhibit correlations of user behavior.

There was a positive correlation between engagement rates of power users and the frequency of their purchases.

I analyzed highly engaged users’ entry points to SHFT product ecosystem, the online courses that they participated in as well as the frequency of purchases and relevant demographic data.There was a positive correlation between engagement rates of power users and the timeline of their purchases. So we had to look at ways to increase engagement and incentivize purchases.


Reply / comment structure

Users frequently mentioned in their interviews that they had distracted reading all the other members’ comments and due to this feedback my initial design solution was to use a flat comment structure to compartmentalize the conversations. This design structure did not perform well in actual usage due to the length of time required to navigate from separate comments and reply pages - the perceived speed was slow so the I redesigned a threaded structure to increase the perceived speed of the SHFT platform in order to boost engagement rates which is a key metric for the company.


Checkout Page / Coupon code

After discovering the correlation between purchase frequencies and becoming a power user, we created a bundle of the most common online courses sent personalized coupons to incentivize ongoing users.


Results

Overall user retention increased by 10% due to personalized coupon codes and improved comment structure which increased the engagement and cutbacked user drop offs.
