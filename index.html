HTML / CSS

Project:

Designing a homepage for SHFT life coaching course.

Problem:

Getting more qualified leads for the sales team and increase the conversion rates and automating the process.

Solution:

Creating a content strategy based on user insights projected on customer journey map.

Avarage time to purchase

I partnered with the marketing department to analyse the typical length for a visitor to become a paid customer for the SHFT life coaching course and the result was more than a few months. It showed that a customer interacts with the product at multiple touch points with the product with different information needs. This metric shaped my design principles different than a regular immediate sales focused ecommerce approach.

User journey map

In order to create a journey map focused on different information needs of potential users me and my team interviewed a few users and gathered insights on what they looked for before signing up. My main focus was finding the information needs for pre and post purchase periods. I validated this information with the interviews I conducted with the sales team by gathering the most asked questions during the sales process.

Content blue print

After conducting the user interviews I provided the marketing & sales team with the blueprint of the content headlines and reference points.

Results

Redesiged homepage increased the conversion by %13.8 With the findings of user interviews and journey maps product team developed a drip campaing and a webinar.

Project:

Redesigning the homepage for SHFT online group coaching products.

Problem:

SHFT is a multifaceted product providing online group coaching sessions, users had difficulty understanding the scope and breadth of the product. These clarification issues led to high bounce rates that resulted in lower conversion rates.

Solution:

Create the core persona for SHFT and create content with rapid user evaluation and replacing the content until reaching the desired clarity scores.

Persona creation

Upon joining SHFT, a 3 month old start-up at the time, I began analyzing the data of the existing SHFT Facebook community members, which was the main entry point to the Group Coaching product and created a main persona for the product.

User Interviews

After conducting enough user interviews to reach a relevant conclusion, I found that users who visited the home page did not understand the product from our description, additionally they misunderstood how the free trial version worked as well as how much time, money and commitment was needed from them to participate.

User Testing

After redesigning a new draft of the homepage based on user feedback, I decided to test the new homepage with remote users, who were unfamiliar with the product,before spending any additional marketing money. I devised a clarity score that addressed the problems pattern above and worked closely with the marketing team to fine tune the copy based on our findings during user testing. I separately pointed the testing results after watching the recorded videos and calculated an average number for every version. After 36 remote users tests and 5 iterations I was were ready to put up the page in front of real users.

Results

Increased understanding of the multifacted product ecosystem from existing users as well as a substantial overall increase in user acquisition from 4% conversion to 15% conversion for cold traffic.

Project:

Design a customizable, functional profile page for SHFT life coaches to market themselves, handle payments, and for clients to begin the scheduling process.

Goal

Design a customizable, functional profile page for SHFT life coaches to market themselves, handle payments, and for clients to begin the scheduling process.

Solution:

Designed a template profile page and checkout process that allowed coaches to overcome their lack of technical expertise, and begin generating revenue immediately. Profile pages also allowed SHFT to lay the groundwork for, and begin to generate revenue, via a client and coaching marketplace. Also eliminated the need for coaches to take their services off platform or to build their own websites.

User Interviews

I conducted interviews with the life coaches working with SHFT.

My Findings were

  • SHFT life coaches were not incredibly tech savvy, and building a website was a huge blocker in starting their online coaching business and finding clients.

  • Coaches wanted their website to be customizable, with individual pricing plans and testimonials.

  • Coaches wanted clients to be able to learn about them via their bio and other uniqueness indicators.
